1. Let’s-ParaConnect Platform Overview

Let’s-ParaConnect is a professional marketplace designed to allow attorneys to engage independent paralegals for project-based legal support. The Platform enables attorneys to post cases and allows approved paralegals to apply for and perform discrete assignments as independent contractors. Let’s-ParaConnect provides the technology infrastructure that facilitates these professional collaborations. Let’s-ParaConnect does not provide legal services, legal representation, or legal advice.

1.1 Flat-Fee Cases

Flat-fee cases allow attorneys and paralegals to collaborate on clearly defined assignments with a fixed price. Examples may include:

  • drafting deeds
  • legal research assignments
  • preparing pleadings
  • document review
  • preparing contracts or filings

All work performed through the Platform is performed by independent professionals, and the hiring attorney retains full responsibility for supervising the work and ensuring compliance with applicable professional conduct rules.

2. Flat-Fee Cases

2.1 Overview of How Flat-Fee Cases Work

An attorney may post a discrete assignment (a “Case”) to the Platform. Each Case posting includes, among other information:

  • a description of the requested work
  • relevant deadlines
  • required tasks
  • the flat-fee amount offered for completion of the Case

The minimum case compensation on the Platform is $400.

Approved paralegals may apply to the Case. When a paralegal applies, their profile is presented to the posting attorney for review. The attorney may review applicants and select a paralegal to perform the work. At that point, the attorney and paralegal are provided access to collaboration tools within the Platform, which may include:

  • secure messaging
  • document sharing
  • case-related workflow features

The Attorney and Paralegal will be provided access to the Platform’s collaboration tools, which may include secure messaging, document sharing, and case-related workflow features designed to facilitate completion of the Case. Attorneys may also invite Paralegals to apply to Cases directly through the Platform or through the Platform’s directory of approved Paralegals.

2.2 Attorney Payment for Flat-Fee Cases

When an Attorney selects a Paralegal for a flat-fee Case, the Attorney authorizes payment for the Case through the Platform. Attorneys must provide valid payment information prior to hiring a Paralegal for a Case. Payment information is managed through the payment settings section of the Attorney’s account dashboard. By posting and hiring for a flat-fee Case through the Platform, the Attorney authorizes Let’s-ParaConnect, directly or through its third-party payment processor, to process payment for the Case amount using the Attorney’s designated payment method. Payments are processed in accordance with the Platform’s payment workflow and the terms of the applicable payment processor.

2.3 Paralegal Payment for Flat-Fee Cases

Upon timely completion of a Case, the paralegal will be paid the Case amount presented at the time the paralegal applied for or accepted the Case. Payments are issued to the paralegal’s designated payout account as provided during the payment onboarding process within the paralegal’s account dashboard. Payments are processed through the Platform’s payment processor and are released upon the later of: (i) the hiring attorney’s confirmation of completion within the Case dashboard; or (ii) the expiration of the applicable review period following submission of the final deliverable, provided the paralegal or attorney have not requested review within that period. Depending on the financial institution used by the paralegal, funds may take several business days to appear in the paralegal’s bank account after the payout is initiated. Let’s-ParaConnect does not take custody of user funds and does not provide banking services. All payment authorization, settlement, and payout timing are governed by the systems and policies of the applicable payment processor.

2.4 Rules Governing the Use of Paralegals for Case Work

For each Case posted on the Platform, the Attorney agrees to comply with the following rules governing the use of Paralegals:

  • The Attorney retains sole professional responsibility for all legal work and work product associated with the Case.
  • The Attorney will supervise the Paralegal’s work to ensure compliance with all applicable laws and Rules of Professional Conduct.
  • Unless permitted by applicable professional conduct rules, Paralegals shall not communicate directly with the Attorney’s clients, opposing counsel, witnesses, or other individuals involved in the legal matter.
  • Paralegals shall not appear in court or before any judicial or administrative body on behalf of the Attorney’s client.
  • Paralegals shall not sign or file documents with any court or administrative body on behalf of the Attorney’s client.
  • Where required by law or by the Attorney’s engagement agreement with their client, the Attorney is responsible for obtaining any necessary client consent for the use of paralegal assistance.
  • The Attorney retains sole responsibility for determining the fees charged to the Attorney’s clients for legal services.
  • All payments between Attorneys and Paralegals for work performed through the Platform must be processed through the Let’s-ParaConnect Platform in accordance with these Terms.

2.5 Let’s-ParaConnect Platform Fee for Flat-Fee Cases

Let’s-ParaConnect does not provide legal services and does not charge fees for legal services. In exchange for providing the Platform and related services, Let’s-ParaConnect charges a platform fee in connection with completed Cases. The platform fee is applied to the Case amount in accordance with the fee structure disclosed to users during the Case posting and acceptance process. Platform fees are collected and processed through the Platform’s payment processor as part of the Case payment workflow. Let’s-ParaConnect reserves the right to modify platform fees prospectively. Any changes will apply only to Cases posted after such changes become effective.

3. Paralegal Replacement and Early Termination

3.1 Dispute Review for Flat-Fee Cases

Any dispute relating to a flat-fee Case must be initiated through the Platform prior to payment release. Let’s-ParaConnect shall make a final determination in its sole and absolute discretion as to how the dispute will be resolved based upon the information provided by the Attorney and Paralegal that are party to the dispute, taking into consideration: (i) the clarity of the Case description posted by the Attorney; (ii) whether the nature and quality of the Paralegal’s services rendered in connection with the Case were consistent with industry standards; (iii) whether the initial and final deadlines were met; (iv) whether a scope change was requested and the extent and nature of such scope change; (v) whether revisions were requested by the Attorney and the extent and nature of such revisions; (vi) whether the parties to the dispute have complied with this Agreement; and (vii) any other applicable fact or consideration that may be relevant to Let’s-ParaConnect’s determination of the dispute.

3.2 Missed Deadlines

Paralegals are expected to meet all deadlines agreed upon within a Case unless an extension has been requested and approved by the hiring Attorney through the Platform.

A “missed deadline” occurs when a Paralegal fails to submit the required deliverable by the agreed deadline and has not received approval from the Attorney for an extension.

If a Paralegal misses a deadline and no work product has been delivered, the hiring Attorney may request review through the Platform and may be eligible for a full refund of the Case amount.

Repeated missed deadlines may result in disciplinary action by Let’s-ParaConnect, including warnings, temporary restrictions, probation, suspension, or termination of the Paralegal’s account.

As a general guideline, a pattern of three (3) missed deadlines may result in suspension or termination of the Paralegal’s access to the Platform at Let’s-ParaConnect’s sole discretion.

Because deadlines are a material part of Case work performed through the Platform, unless a Paralegal can establish that the Paralegal was unable to complete a Case by the agreed deadline due to the Attorney’s inaction, failure to provide necessary information, or other circumstances outside the Paralegal’s control, a dispute based on a missed deadline may be resolved in favor of the Attorney and payment for the Case may be refunded in full through the Platform’s payment processor.

5) Fees, Taxes & Chargebacks

  • Platform Fees. Platform fees are disclosed in the case posting and acceptance flow. We may update fees prospectively.
  • Processing Fees. Payment processing fees are governed by Stripe’s terms.
  • Taxes. Each user is responsible for determining and paying applicable taxes. The Company does not provide tax advice.
  • Chargebacks & Reversals. If a transaction is reversed or subject to chargeback under payment network rules, Stripe may debit or offset amounts from balances or future payouts.
  • Cancellations & Case Deletion. Attorneys may delete a case only before a paralegal is hired and before Stripe funding occurs. Once a case is funded or a paralegal is hired, deletion is not available; cases may be archived for recordkeeping.
  • Withdrawals & Dispute Window. If a paralegal withdraws before completion, the case is paused. If no tasks were completed, a $0 payout is issued and the case relists. If some work was completed, the attorney may propose a partial payout consistent with the work completed. Any payout adjustments are processed through Stripe in accordance with Stripe’s rules. If the attorney closes without release, this starts a 24-hour dispute window for the withdrawn paralegal. Disputes are reviewed for compliance with Platform policies. Any resulting refund or payout adjustments are processed through Stripe in accordance with Stripe’s rules and payment network requirements.
  • Refunds. Refunds (full or partial) are issued only through the Platform’s dispute process before funds are released and are processed by Stripe to the original payment method. After payout release, refunds are generally not available through the Platform, though chargebacks or network adjustments may occur.
